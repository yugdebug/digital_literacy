# Case Study: The Payment Received Paradox (UPI QR Scam)

### **What the Crime Is**
This scenario falls under UPI/Online Payment Fraud. It is a social engineering attack where a criminal manipulates the victim into authorizing  and giving an outgoing payment under the guise of receiving funds. Unlike technical hacking, this Reverse Scam relies entirely on exploiting the victim's lack of digital literacy regarding how UPI works.

### **How It Happens (Step-by-Step)**
1. **The Bait:** An attacker poses as a buyer on a marketplace like a student Buy/Sell group and showing immediate interest in an item you've listed online.
2. **The Hook:** They insist on paying via UPI to reserve the item building a false sense of urgency for you.
3. **The Deception:** They send a QR code via WhatsApp claiming you must scan this to receive the money.
4. **The Error:** When you scan the code your app asks for your **UPI PIN**. The attacker often stays on the call distracting you with his talk.
5. **The Theft:** The moment you enter your PIN, you aren't confirming a deposit.You are digitally signing an outgoing transaction. The money vanishes instantly from your bank.

### **Target Audience & Consequences**
College students are the primary targets because they are frequent users of upi payments apps and often engage in second hand buying and selling. The consequences go beyond the immediate financial loss for many, it leads to trauma causing them to distrust legitimate tools. Furthermore, recovering funds from UPI is difficult because the transaction is given in the form of pin by the user.

---
> **Ambassador's Safety Note:** Remember, you **NEVER** need to enter your PIN to receive money. If an app asks for a PIN during a deposit, it’s a trap.Report such incidents at **cybercrime.gov.in** or call **1930**.
